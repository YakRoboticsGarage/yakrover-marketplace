## Part 2: Market Context

### FAA Part 107 in Michigan
- **National total:** 368,604 Part 107 certificates issued as of end-2023, growing ~50K/year. Estimated ~420,000+ by early 2026.
- **Michigan estimate:** ~3-4% of national total based on population share = **~12,000-16,000 Part 107 holders in Michigan.** Most are hobbyists or occasional commercial users. Estimated 200-400 active commercial drone survey operators statewide.
- **Training pipeline:** WMU (Kalamazoo), MSU (Lansing), and NMC all offer Part 107 programs.

### Michigan Society of Professional Surveyors (MSPS)
- **Website:** [misps.org](https://www.misps.org/)
- **Note:** The correct name is MSPS, not MAPS. They have a "Find a Surveyor" directory. Contact: 517-484-2413, centraloffice@misps.org.
- **Opportunity:** MSPS members are traditional survey firms -- the ones most likely to have added drone capability while retaining PLS credentials. A presentation or booth at an MSPS event would reach the highest-value operator segment.

### MDOT Drone Program
- MDOT has been developing drone capabilities since 2013 in partnership with Michigan Tech.
- Phase III project focused on: traffic operations, bridge inspection, construction inspection, LiDAR UAS for design survey.
- **Key partner:** Surveying Solutions Inc. (SSI) -- already in our Tier A list.
- MDOT estimated traditional bridge deck inspection costs $4,600 vs. $1,000 with a drone (78% savings).
- Michigan Central and MDOT launched Detroit's Advanced Aerial Innovation Region (3-mile radius around Michigan Central).
- **Implication:** MDOT is drone-friendly. GCs bidding on MDOT projects can use drone survey data. This validates demand.

### Existing Marketplace Platforms
- **Zeitview (fka DroneBase):** 60,000+ pilot network, nationwide. They have Michigan coverage but focus on real estate/solar/insurance -- not construction survey. Operators on Zeitview may be underserved for construction demand.
- **DroneDeploy:** Software platform, not an operator marketplace. But Drone Brothers uses it, meaning their pilots are digitally sophisticated.
- **Droners.io:** Freelance pilot marketplace. Emmet Drones (Kurtis Damerow) has a profile there -- indicates willingness to list on platforms.

## Part 3: Operator Interview Guide

> **Purpose:** Discovery, not recruitment. Understand their business model before pitching anything.
> **Duration:** 20-30 minutes. Phone or video.
> **Tone:** Peer conversation between people who understand the business. NOT a sales call.

### Opening (2 min)
"Hi [name], I'm [your name] from yakrobot.bid. We're building a marketplace that connects construction GCs with drone survey operators. I'm not trying to sell you anything today -- I'm trying to understand how your business actually works so we can build something operators would actually use. Do you have 20 minutes?"

### Business Model Questions (10 min)

1. **"How do you find new clients right now?"**
   - Listen for: word of mouth, cold outreach, Zeitview/DroneBase, referrals from survey firms, repeat clients
   - Follow-up: "What percentage of your revenue comes from repeat clients vs. new?"

2. **"What's your utilization rate? How many days per month are you actually flying?"**
   - Listen for: 8-12 days = healthy; under 8 = has capacity; over 15 = very busy
   - Follow-up: "Is that limited by demand or by weather/logistics?"

3. **"What equipment are you running?"**
   - Listen for: M350 RTK + L2/P1 = professional; Mavic 3 Enterprise = mid-tier; consumer DJI = entry
   - Follow-up: "What processing software? Pix4D? DroneDeploy? Terra?"

4. **"What do you charge? Per acre, per day, or per project?"**
   - Listen for: $30-80/acre photogrammetry, $50-120/acre LiDAR, $1,000-3,000/day
   - Follow-up: "How do you set rates? Market research, or gut feel?"

5. **"What deliverables do your clients actually ask for?"**
   - Listen for: orthomosaics, DTMs, point clouds, cut/fill reports, progress photos
   - Follow-up: "Do they need PLS-stamped deliverables, or just raw data?"

### Pain Point Questions (8 min)

6. **"What's the most frustrating part of running this business?"**
   - Listen for: finding clients, pricing pressure, weather delays, payment delays, insurance hassles
   - This is the golden question. Let them talk.

7. **"How long does it take to get paid after you deliver?"**
   - Listen for: net-30, net-60, "I've chased invoices for months"
   - Follow-up: "Have you ever not gotten paid for completed work?"

8. **"What would you do with 5 more billable days per month?"**
   - Listen for: "I'd take them," "I'd need to hire," "I'm already maxed"
   - This gauges capacity and appetite.

### Marketplace Reaction (5 min)

9. **"If a platform brought you qualified leads with verified payment through escrow, would you list your services?"**
   - Listen for: enthusiasm, skepticism, specific objections
   - Follow-up to objections: acknowledge, don't argue

10. **"What would make you NOT want to use a marketplace?"**
    - Listen for: commission too high, loss of control, exclusivity requirement, data ownership concerns, platform dictates pricing
    - This tells you what NOT to build.

11. **Show yakrobot.bid** (if on video) or describe the "For Operators" value prop.
    - "Here's what we're building. What's your gut reaction?"
    - Listen more than you talk.

### Closing (2 min)
"This was incredibly helpful. Can I follow up when we have the operator dashboard ready for beta? No commitment -- just a first look."

## Part 4: Operator Value Proposition One-Pager

### For Operators: More Jobs, Guaranteed Payment, Zero Lock-In

**The #1 reason drone survey businesses fail is finding clients. We solve that.**

#### What yakrobot.bid Does For You

**Demand pipeline.** Construction GCs post survey tasks -- topo, volumetrics, progress monitoring, as-built. You see jobs in your area and bid on the ones that match your equipment and schedule. No cold calls. No networking events. No waiting for referrals.

**Verified payment.** Every task has funds in escrow before you fly. When you deliver, you get paid. No net-60 invoices. No chasing GCs for checks. No "the check is in the mail." Escrow releases on delivery acceptance, with dispute resolution if needed.

**Your rates, your terms.** You set your own prices. You accept or decline jobs. The marketplace suggests rates based on local market data, but you decide. We never undercut you or assign work at rates you didn't approve.

#### What We Don't Require

- **No exclusivity.** Keep your direct clients. Use the marketplace for overflow, gap-filling, or new market entry.
- **No equipment purchase.** Bring what you already own. We qualify your gear for eligible task types.
- **No monthly fee.** We take a commission on completed tasks only. If you don't work, you don't pay.
- **No data surrender.** Your client relationships are yours. We don't sell your client list or contact your clients directly.

#### How It Works

1. **Create your operator profile.** Upload Part 107, insurance COI, equipment list. One-time verification.
2. **Browse open tasks.** See construction survey jobs in your area with scope, budget, and deadline.
3. **Bid on what fits.** Submit your price and availability. The AI matching system considers your equipment, reputation, and proximity.
4. **Fly and deliver.** Complete the task, upload deliverables. Processing pipeline handles format conversion.
5. **Get paid.** Escrow releases to your account. Direct deposit or USDC -- your choice.

#### Who We're Looking For

| Equipment Tier | Eligible Tasks | Expected Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| Mavic 3 Enterprise / Autel EVO II RTK | Small-site photogrammetry, progress photos | $1,000-$1,800/day |
| M350 RTK + L2 or P1 | Topo survey, volumetrics, DOT corridors | $2,000-$3,000/day |
| Multi-sensor (LiDAR + photogrammetry + thermal) | Everything including as-built, inspection | $2,500-$4,000/day |

#### The Math

A solo operator flying 12 days/month at $2,200/day average = **$26,400/month gross**. After marketplace commission, software, insurance, and vehicle costs = **~$20,000/month net**. Equipment payback in under 2 months.

The marketplace doesn't replace your business. It fills your calendar.
