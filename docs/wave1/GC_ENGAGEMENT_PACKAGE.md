## Section 3: Discovery Interview Guide

**Goal:** Understand how they buy surveys today. NOT a sales pitch. You are researching how the industry works. If they feel sold to, you lose the relationship.

**Duration:** 25-30 minutes. Respect their time -- estimators are the busiest people at a GC.

**Opening (2 min):**
> "Thanks for taking the time. We're building a platform that connects contractors with drone survey operators, and before we build anything else, we want to understand how firms like yours actually handle survey procurement today. I'm not here to pitch -- I genuinely want to learn how your process works."

### Block 1: Current Process (10 min)

**Q1 (Open-ended):** "Walk me through what happens when you need a site survey for a bid. From the moment you realize you need one to when you have data in hand."

*Listen for:* Who initiates? How many calls? How long to get a quote? How long to schedule? Who processes the data?

**Q2 (Probing):** "How many survey firms do you typically reach out to for a single bid?"

*Listen for:* One trusted firm vs. shopping around. Geographic coverage issues.

**Q3 (Capacity):** "During peak season -- March through October, letting season -- how often do you have trouble getting a crew scheduled when you need one?"

*Listen for:* Frequency. Which months are worst. Have they ever missed a bid because of survey timing?

**Q4 (In-house vs. sub):** "Do you have in-house survey capability, or is it all subcontracted?"

*Listen for:* If in-house, how many crews? Are they always on active projects (unavailable for pre-bid)? If sub, how many firms on their list?

### Block 2: Pain Points (8 min)

**Q5 (Frustration):** "What's the most frustrating part of that process?"

*Listen for:* Turnaround time, scheduling conflicts, data format issues, cost unpredictability, crew no-shows.

**Q6 (Cost):** "Roughly, what does a typical pre-bid site survey cost you? And how many do you commission per quarter?"

*Listen for:* Dollar range ($3K-$15K typical). Volume (5-20 per quarter for a $100M+ firm). Annual spend calculation.

**Q7 (Data quality):** "When the survey data comes back, how often does it need rework or reprocessing before it fits into your estimating software?"

*Listen for:* Format mismatches. Coordinate system issues. Missing cross-sections. Time spent on data cleanup.

**Q8 (Missed bids):** "Have you ever had to pass on a bid -- or submit with less confidence -- because survey data wasn't available in time?"

*Listen for:* This is the money question. If yes, quantify: how many per year, what size contracts?

**Q8b (Value of speed):** "If you could get that survey data back in 48 hours instead of 2-3 weeks — same quality, PLS-stamped, Civil 3D-ready — what would that be worth to your bidding operation? Not the survey cost itself, but the speed."

*Listen for:* Let them name the number. Don't suggest a price. If they say "I'd pay a premium for that" — ask how much. If they say "that'd save me two missed bids a year" — that's $5-20M in potential contracts. The 12% platform fee ($8,600 on a $72K survey) should feel trivial against that number. If it doesn't, the value prop isn't landing.

### Block 3: Reaction to the Concept (8 min)

**Q9 (Value prop):** "What if you could describe what you need in plain English -- 'I need topo for a 12-acre highway widening, data by Saturday' -- and get competitive bids from certified drone operators within two hours?"

*Listen for:* Skepticism points. "Who certifies them?" "What about accuracy?" "My data needs to go into HeavyBid/Civil 3D."

**Q10 (Demo):** "Let me show you something quick -- this is yakrobot.bid."

*Walk them through:* Post a task, see bids come in, see deliverable formats. Keep it under 3 minutes.

**Q11 (Willingness):** "If this existed today, would you try it on your next bid?"

*Listen for:* Conditional yes ("if the accuracy is there," "if they're licensed," "if it integrates with our tools"). Absolute yes. Hesitation (why?).

### Close (2 min)

> "This is incredibly helpful. Two last things:
> 1. Would you be open to trying this on a real project? We'd cover the first survey so you can evaluate the quality with zero risk.
> 2. Is there anyone else in your network -- another estimator, a preconstruction VP -- who'd be worth talking to?"

**After the call:** Send a thank-you email within 2 hours. Reference one specific thing they said. Do NOT attach a pitch deck.

## Section 4: Team Cheat Sheet -- How GCs Hire Survey Firms Today

**One page. Read before your first call.**

### The Buying Process

1. **GC receives an RFP or identifies a project** at an upcoming MDOT letting (published quarterly on michigan.gov/mdot).
2. **Estimator reviews plan sheets** -- the engineering drawings that define the project scope. Plan sheets include cross-sections, alignment data, and existing condition assumptions.
3. **Estimator determines survey needs:** Does the existing data (from MDOT or the engineer of record) cover the site? Usually not fully -- GCs need their own field-verified data to price earthwork confidently.
4. **Estimator calls 1-3 survey firms** they've used before. In Michigan, these are typically small firms (5-15 people) with a PLS (Professional Licensed Surveyor) on staff.
5. **Survey firm quotes $3K-$15K** depending on corridor length, terrain, and deliverables needed. Turnaround: 5-10 business days.
6. **Crew goes to the field** with a total station and/or GPS rovers. 2-person crew for 1-3 days depending on site size.
7. **Data is processed** in the survey firm's office. Point clouds become terrain models, cross-sections, and plan overlays. This takes 3-5 additional business days.
8. **Estimator imports data** into HCSS HeavyBid, B2W Estimate, or Autodesk Civil 3D for quantity takeoff.
9. **Total elapsed time:** 10-15 business days from request to usable data.

### Key Vocabulary

| Term | What It Means | When You'll Hear It |
|------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Letting** | The date MDOT opens sealed bids for a project. Published in advance. | "The next letting is April 4th." |
| **Bid tab** | The published results showing all bidders and their prices. Public record. | "Check the bid tab to see who won." |
| **Plan sheets** | Engineering drawings for the project (cross-sections, alignment, drainage). | "Have you looked at the plan sheets yet?" |
| **Cross-sections** | Vertical slices across the road alignment showing existing ground vs. design grade. Critical for earthwork quantities. | "I need cross-sections at 50-foot stations." |
| **Control survey** | Establishing precise reference points (benchmarks) before any other survey work begins. | "We need control established before the crew goes out." |
| **PLS** | Professional Licensed Surveyor. Required to stamp and certify survey work. Michigan license. | "Is your operator working under a PLS?" |
| **Part 107** | FAA remote pilot certificate. Required for commercial drone operations. | "All our operators are Part 107 certified." |
| **Topo / topographic survey** | Mapping the existing ground surface -- elevations, contours, features. | "I just need basic topo for the corridor." |
| **DTM** | Digital Terrain Model. The 3D surface generated from survey data. | "Can you deliver the DTM in LandXML?" |
| **Cut/fill** | Earthwork calculation: how much dirt to remove (cut) or add (fill) to reach design grade. | "What are your cut/fill volumes?" |
| **Staking / construction staking** | Placing physical stakes in the ground to guide construction equipment. | "We need staking before the crew mobilizes." |
| **As-built** | Survey of completed construction to verify it matches the design. Required for closeout. | "We need as-builts before final payment." |
| **Pay application / pay app** | Monthly invoice from GC to owner, documenting work completed. Survey data backs the quantities claimed. | "I need the progress data for this month's pay app." |
| **Retainage** | Owner withholds 5-10% of each payment until project completion. | "Retainage won't release until as-builts are accepted." |
| **DBE** | Disadvantaged Business Enterprise. Federal/state programs requiring GCs to subcontract a percentage to certified DBE firms. | "Do you have DBE certification?" |

### What NOT to Say

| Instead of... | Say... | Why |
|--------------|--------|-----|
| "Robot" | "Drone survey" or "automated survey" | GCs think robots = factory arms. "Drone" is familiar and credible. |
| "AI agent" | "Our platform" or "the system" | AI hype triggers skepticism. They care about results, not architecture. |
| "Blockchain" / "USDC" / "crypto" | "Secure payment" or "escrow" | Instant credibility killer in construction. Talk payment bonds and escrow. |
| "Disrupting the survey industry" | "Giving you more options for survey crews" | They like their survey firms. You're adding capacity, not replacing anyone. |
| "Autonomous" | "Automated" or "efficient" | Autonomous implies no human oversight. In construction, that's a liability concern. |
| "Marketplace" | "Platform" or "network" | Marketplace sounds like a bazaar. Platform sounds like a tool. |
| "Our algorithm scores bids" | "We match you with qualified operators" | They want to know someone vetted the crew, not that a formula chose them. |

### Numbers Worth Knowing

- Michigan MDOT 5-year program (2026-2030): billions in planned highway/bridge work
- I-94 Modernization (Detroit): $2B+ program, 70+ bridges, 7 miles of interstate rebuild
- Marshall Modernization (I-94/I-69): multi-billion dollar interchange program
- Michigan has 11,000+ bridges. ~1,200 in poor condition.
- Survey crew shortage: Michigan construction workforce report (2025) identifies skilled trades shortage as defining factor through next decade
- 67% of major US construction firms already use drones on projects
- Drone survey cost: $1,500-$3,000 for a 100-acre corridor vs. $8,000-$10,000 for a human crew

## Section 6: Key Decision Makers to Find

For each target company, look for these roles (in priority order):

1. **Chief Estimator / Senior Estimator** -- This is Marco. They feel the survey bottleneck daily. They have budget authority ($5K-$15K per bid for pre-construction services). They decide which survey firms to call.

2. **VP of Preconstruction / Director of Preconstruction** -- Marco's boss. They see the pattern across all bids. They approve larger survey expenditures and new vendor relationships.

3. **Project Manager (active MDOT project)** -- Cares about progress monitoring surveys during construction. Monthly recurring need. Different entry point than pre-bid.

4. **Controller / CFO** -- Signs off on new vendor accounts. Cares about cost savings and payment terms. Not your first call, but you'll need them for the pilot.

### Where to Find Them

- **LinkedIn:** Search "[Company Name]" + "estimator" or "preconstruction"
- **AGC Michigan member directory:** agcmichigan.org -- many of these firms are members

## Section 7: Michigan-Specific Context

### MDOT Letting Schedule

MDOT holds bid lettings roughly monthly. The schedule is published at michigan.gov/mdot/business/contractors/bid-letting. Spring lettings (March-June) are the busiest -- this is when survey demand peaks and crews are hardest to book.

### I-94 Corridor Relevance

The I-94 corridor from Detroit to Kalamazoo is the epicenter of Michigan DOT spending right now:
- **I-94 Modernization (Detroit):** $2B+ program, construction 2028-2031
- **Marshall Modernization (I-94/I-69):** Multi-billion dollar interchange rebuild
- **I-94 Reconstruction (Dearborn):** $353M
- **I-94 Drainage Tunnel:** Design-build, construction late 2026/early 2027

Any GC bidding on I-94 work will need extensive survey data. This is our geographic sweet spot.

### MDOT Survey Requirements

MDOT Chapter 4 defines survey types for highway projects: topographic, control, alignment, hydraulic. All survey work must be performed under a Michigan PLS. Our platform operators must carry this credential. This is a qualification filter, not a nice-to-have.

### Payment Context

Michigan law (MCL 129.201) requires payment bonds on public projects over $50K. MDOT projects always have payment bonds. GCs must pay subs within 10 days of receiving owner payment (MCL 125.1561). Survey subs expect 30-day payment terms. Our platform's faster payment (24-48 hours after delivery confirmation) is a competitive advantage -- but mention it as a benefit to the survey operator, not to the GC. The GC cares about speed and accuracy, not how fast the drone pilot gets paid.
