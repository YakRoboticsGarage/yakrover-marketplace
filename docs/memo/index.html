Executive Summary

  • Construction spending keeps increasing. Construction surveying alone is an $8B/yr US market. The industry loses $31B/yr to rework, a significant portion traceable to inaccurate or outdated site data. To address the demand, drones are deployed on thousands of job sites and engineering companies are adopting AI tooling to increase speed of execution.
  • But no one has built an agent-runtime procurement layer for physical work. And it’s not just construction that has this limitation. Physical-world tasks can’t be purchased like software, yet.
  • The enabling infrastructure exists today. Dock-deployed drones, fixed-wing platforms with hundred-mile ranges, machine-readable DOT specifications, AI agents with tool-use capabilities, alongside a declining workforce in licensed technical professions like surveying.
  • Robots don’t just replace scarce labor, they widen the feasible action space of agents. Quality becomes computable, availability becomes queryable, and execution data accumulates for quality benchmarking. Each task becomes structurally compatible with algorithmic procurement in a way that human execution alone does not easily support.
  • Yak Robotics already has a proof of concept protocol. Auction engine, robot identity, settlement via Stripe and stablecoin, credential verification, and a 100-robot test fleet (simulated execution).
  • Seeking partners in AI-agent procurement infrastructure, autonomous robotics, vertical domain expertise such as construction, and research in robot navigation and regulation.

Why Now

Physical service procurement is unstructured and unavailable for agents. The technologies necessary to enable API-like purchase of physical actions are emerging. Yak Robotics is building a protocol and application layer for physical-world task procurement — structured task specification, executor matching, delivery verification, and payment settlement.

Five independent trends are converging to make this programmable in ways it hasn’t been before. None is sufficient alone, but together they create the conditions for a protocol layer that did not previously have the infrastructure to exist. As an example, construction survey on Department of Transportation (DOT) highway projects satisfies all necessary conditions with currently available technology.

Autonomous collection: DroneDeploy operates docked drones on 100+ projects (Oct 2025). Komatsu SMARTCONSTRUCTION: 35,000 sites in Japan (Nov 2025). Ukrainian ground robots completed 24,500 frontline missions in Q1 2026 — supply delivery, ammunition transport, casualty evacuation — scaling from 2,900 to 9,000 missions/month in five months.

Digital design deliverables: Japan i-Construction: 86% information and communications technology (ICT) adoption in 6 years via mandate. Norway V770: 50%+ change order reduction with model-based delivery.

Numerical regulatory specs: Michigan Department of Transportation (MDOT) Section 205.03.N: subgrade ±1 inch. Section 302.03.A: aggregate base ±½ inch. Published, deterministic, machine-parseable.

Workforce decline: Average US Professional Land Surveyor (PLS) age: 57-60 (American Society of Civil Engineers / National Society of Professional Surveyors). 51% of engineering firms turning down work (American Council of Engineering Companies, Q4 2024). 8-year licensing pipeline. Similar trends reported internationally.

Agent tool-use: Model Context Protocol (MCP, Anthropic 2024): growing ecosystem of tool servers. Agent-mediated procurement of physical services does not exist today. The infrastructure is being built.

Protocol

Components and Status (v1.4.1 — Alpha, Functional)

The protocol is implemented as seven modules. The verification engine is the domain-specific component; all others are domain-agnostic and shared across verticals.

Coordination Layer: MCP-native interface, reverse auction engine (11-state lifecycleBidding → Bid Accepted → In Progress → Delivered → Verified → Settled. Plus: Withdrawn, Rejected, Expired, Provider Cancelled, and re-pool back to Bidding., 4-factor scoringPrice (40%) + SLA commitment (25%) + AI confidence (20%) + Reputation (15%). Bids above budget ceiling excluded. See AD-6., geographic filtering). Deployed on Fly.io. Alpha, functional.

Task Registry: 18 task categoriestopo_survey, aerial_survey, site_survey, bridge_inspection, progress_monitoring, as_built, subsurface_scan, env_sensing, visual_inspection, thermal_inspection, corridor_survey, volumetric, confined_space, utility_detection, mapping, delivery_ground, environmental_survey, control_survey. defined with structured capability requirements (ASPRS accuracy classes, CRS codes, sensors, deliverables). RFP to spec conversion. Alpha, functional.

Executor Registry: On-chain robot identity (ERC-8004 standard on Base). Discovery via The Graph indexing protocol. Ethereum Attestation Service (EAS) for platform attestation. 1 physical robot on mainnet + 100 test registrations on testnet. Alpha.

Credential Verification: SAM.gov Exclusions API and bond verification against Treasury Circular 570 (500+ sureties) built as standalone modules, not yet integrated into auction flow.

Verification Engine: 4-levelLevel 0: None (buyer trusts vendor). Level 1: Basic (data exists, format valid, fields present). Level 2: Standards (point density, accuracy, CRS per ASPRS/USGS). Level 3: PLS review required before payment. quality assurance framework with 8 delivery schemasAerial LiDAR, Aerial Photo, Aerial Thermal, Ground GPR, Ground LiDAR, Bridge Inspection, Corridor, Confined Space — each with category-specific required fields, accuracy metrics, and file format validation. mapped across task categories. Tested with simulated data. Geospatial surface comparison is next phase.

Settlement Layer: Stripe (authorize/capture), Connect Express, ACH bank transfer, USDC stablecoin on Base (gasless). 4-mode abstractionImmediate Transparent (Base x402, v1.5). Immediate Private (Horizen L3 candidate, v2.1). Batched Transparent (DTN lunar, v2.1). Batched Private (convergence, v3.0). Currently card, bank, and stablecoin settlement are operational. The 4 modes formalize the timing × privacy design space. designed to cover timing and privacy dimensions as the protocol scales.

Robot Federation: MCP-to-MCP robot adapter with 9 category simulatorsaerial-lidar, aerial-photo, aerial-thermal, ground-gpr, ground-lidar, confined, fixedwing, skydio, fakerover — each deployed on Fly.io as a standalone MCP server returning category-specific structured delivery data. on Fly.io for mock execution. Dynamic tool resolution. 1 physical robot connected (Tumbller via MCP). No production survey robots yet.

Open Challenges

Privacy: Construction RFPs contain sensitive site data, bid pricing, and project timelines. Government contracts add ITAR/CUI restrictions. The protocol must handle task matching without the platform seeing task content, and settle payments without revealing amounts to third parties. EU Anti-Money Laundering Regulation Article 79 constrains which privacy approaches are legally viable.

Regulation: FAA Part 108 (Beyond Visual Line of Sight) final rule expected mid-2026, following Executive Order 14307 directing routine commercial BVLOS operations. This unlocks autonomous drone dispatch without per-flight waivers — a prerequisite for algorithmic procurement at scale. Ground robots currently lack standardized navigation and right-of-way rules on construction sites.

Scale: Operator supply density uncertain in rural corridors. Credential APIs fragmented (50 state PLS boards). Trust bootstrapping requires managed curation (Brooks Act QBS culture). MDOT drone data acceptance not yet standard.

Liability: When an autonomously dispatched robot causes damage, liability allocation between manufacturer, operator, platform, and software provider is unsettled law. Robotics-specific insurance is emerging (AXIS, Munich Re), but adoption stalls when liability is unclear. Proportional frameworks — where responsibility scales with autonomy level — are proposed but not yet codified.
