Executive Summary

  • US surveying and mapping services is an $11.5 billion per year market (IBISWorld, 2025). The US construction industry loses $31 billion per year to rework from poor project data and miscommunication (PlanGrid/FMI, 2018). Drones are deployed on thousands of job sites and engineering firms are adopting AI tooling to accelerate execution.
  • But no one has built a procurement protocol that lets a software agent hire a physical robot. Physical-world tasks cannot be purchased like software. This is because the infrastructure to discover, bid, verify, and pay them does not exist yet.
  • The enabling infrastructure exists today: dock-deployed drones, fixed-wing survey platforms with multi-hour endurance, machine-readable DOT specifications, and AI agents with tool-use capabilities — alongside a declining workforce in licensed technical professions like surveying.
  • Robots don’t just replace scarce labor, they widen the feasible action space of agents. Quality becomes computable, availability becomes queryable, and execution data accumulates for quality benchmarking. Each task becomes structurally compatible with algorithmic procurement in a way that human execution alone does not easily support.
  • Yak Robotics has a working proof of concept: auction engine, on-chain robot identity, settlement via Stripe and stablecoin, credential verification, and a 100-robot test fleet with simulated execution.
  • We are seeking partners in AI-agent procurement infrastructure, autonomous robotics, construction domain expertise, and research in robot navigation and regulation.

Why Now

Physical service procurement today is unstructured and inaccessible to software agents. The infrastructure to change that did not exist five years ago. It does now.

Five trends make this possible now. Autonomous collection platforms are deployed at scale. Design deliverables have gone digital. Regulatory specifications are machine-readable. The technical workforce is declining. And AI agents can use tools. Construction survey on DOT highway projects is the clearest example — every precondition is met with currently available technology.

  • Autonomous collection: DroneDeploy's dock automation platform manages autonomous drone flights on 100+ construction sites (Oct 2025). Komatsu SMARTCONSTRUCTION: deployed across tens of thousands of sites in Japan. Ukrainian ground robot deployments scaled from 2,900 to 9,000 missions per month over five months in 2026 (Scripps News), demonstrating rapid adoption of autonomous ground platforms under operational pressure.
  • Digital design deliverables: Japan i-Construction: 86% ICT adoption on public earthwork projects in 6 years via mandate. Norway V770: 50%+ change order reduction with model-based delivery.
  • Numerical regulatory specs: Michigan Department of Transportation (MDOT) Standard Specifications define sub-inch tolerances for subgrade and aggregate base. Published, deterministic, machine-parseable.
  • Workforce decline: Average US Professional Land Surveyor (PLS) age: 57–60 (NSPS). 51% of engineering firms report workforce-constrained capacity (American Council of Engineering Companies, Q4 2024). 8-year licensing pipeline. Similar trends reported internationally.
  • Agent tool-use: Model Context Protocol (MCP, Anthropic 2024): growing ecosystem of tool servers. Agent-mediated procurement of physical services does not exist today. The infrastructure is being built.

Protocol

Components and Status

The protocol is implemented as seven modules, listed below. The verification engine is domain-specific; all others are domain-agnostic and shared across verticals.

  • Coordination Layer: MCP-native interface, reverse auction engine (11-state lifecycleBidding → Bid Accepted → In Progress → Delivered → Verified → Settled. Plus: Withdrawn, Rejected, Expired, Provider Cancelled, and re-pool back to Bidding., 4-factor scoringPrice (40%) + SLA commitment (25%) + AI confidence (20%) + Reputation (15%). Bids above budget ceiling excluded. See AD-6., geographic filtering). Deployed on Fly.io.
  • Task Registry: 18 task categoriestopo_survey, aerial_survey, site_survey, bridge_inspection, progress_monitoring, as_built, subsurface_scan, env_sensing, visual_inspection, thermal_inspection, corridor_survey, volumetric, confined_space, utility_detection, mapping, delivery_ground, environmental_survey, control_survey. defined with structured capability requirements (ASPRS accuracy classes, CRS codes, sensors, deliverables). RFP to spec conversion.
  • Executor Registry: On-chain robot identity (ERC-8004 standard on Base). Discovery via The Graph indexing protocol. Ethereum Attestation Service (EAS) for platform attestation. 1 physical robot on mainnet + 100 test registrations on testnet.
  • Credential Verification: SAM.gov Exclusions API and bond verification against Treasury Circular 570 (500+ sureties) built as standalone modules, not yet integrated into auction flow.
  • Verification Engine: 4-levelLevel 0: None (buyer trusts vendor). Level 1: Basic (data exists, format valid, fields present). Level 2: Standards (point density, accuracy, CRS per ASPRS/USGS). Level 3: PLS review required before payment. quality assurance framework with 8 delivery schemasAerial LiDAR, Aerial Photo, Aerial Thermal, Ground GPR, Ground LiDAR, Bridge Inspection, Corridor, Confined Space — each with category-specific required fields, accuracy metrics, and file format validation. mapped across task categories. Tested with simulated data. Geospatial surface comparison is next phase.
  • Settlement Layer: Stripe (authorize/capture), Connect Express, ACH bank transfer, USDC stablecoin on Base (gasless). 4-mode abstractionImmediate Transparent (Base x402, v1.5). Immediate Private (Horizen L3 candidate, v2.1). Batched Transparent (DTN lunar, v2.1). Batched Private (convergence, v3.0). Currently card, bank, and stablecoin settlement are operational. The 4 modes formalize the timing × privacy design space. designed to cover timing and privacy dimensions as the protocol scales.
  • Robot Federation: MCP-to-MCP robot adapter with 9 category simulatorsaerial-lidar, aerial-photo, aerial-thermal, ground-gpr, ground-lidar, confined, fixedwing, skydio, fakerover — each deployed on Fly.io as a standalone MCP server returning category-specific structured delivery data. on Fly.io for mock execution. Dynamic tool resolution. 1 physical robot connected (Tumbller via MCP). No production survey robots yet.

Open Challenges

AreaChallengeOpportunity
Privacy Construction RFPs contain sensitive site data, bid pricing, and timelines. Government contracts add ITAR/CUI restrictions. EU AML Regulation Article 79 constrains which privacy approaches are legally viable. Private data matching is solved in healthcare: the Epic Care Everywhere network processes over 100 million patient record exchanges per month by matching across systems without either party seeing the other's full dataset. Construction task matching requires the same pattern: connect buyer to operator without the platform seeing task content. No equivalent exists for physical-world procurement.
Regulation FAA Part 108 (BVLOS) final rule expected mid-2026, per Executive Order 14307. Ground robots lack standardized navigation rules on construction sites. BVLOS enables autonomous drone dispatch without per-flight waivers. This is the regulatory prerequisite for algorithmic procurement at scale. The first compliant marketplace captures the market before incumbents adapt to the new rules.
Scale Operator supply density uncertain in rural corridors. Credential APIs fragmented across 50 state PLS boards. Trust requires managed curation. MDOT drone data acceptance not yet standard. The same cold-start problem Airbnb solved with photography and Craigslist integration. The demand signal system converts failed searches into operator recruitment data: each unmet request shows operators exactly where to invest.
Liability A 2024 federal ruling (360 Virtual Drone Services v. Ritter, 4th Circuit) held that drone survey data constitutes licensed surveying. Autonomous dispatch liability allocation is unsettled. Standard commercial drone insurance is written for piloted operations. Coverage for fully autonomous missions is available but not yet standardized. Amazon accepted liability for third-party seller defects in 2021 and converted it into a trust advantage. The platform that defines the liability framework for autonomous dispatch (data-acquisition subcontractor model, proportional responsibility by autonomy level) writes the standard others adopt.
Software fragmentation Construction is the second-least digitized industry (McKinsey). Poor interoperability costs $15.8 billion per year in 2002 dollars (NIST, 2004). Two-thirds of interoperability costs occur during operations, not construction (NIST). No incumbent integration layer exists to displace. A marketplace that connects to the fragmented toolchain via standardized tool-use becomes the connective tissue the industry lacks. Stripe became the payment layer by integrating with everything rather than replacing anything.

Join Us

  • Agent runtime services: The protocol’s MCP interface is live — an AI agent can post a task, receive ranked bids from 100 robots, and settle payment in a single session. We are looking for teams building agent infrastructure who want to test physical-world tool-use against a working auction engine, not a mock API.

We need operators who can register real survey platforms — dock-deployed drones, ground-based LiDAR rigs, GPR carts — and execute tasks end-to-end through the MCP adapter. We are looking for firms who procure survey services and would share RFP structures, acceptance criteria, and sample data to ground the QA pipeline in production standards rather than simulated benchmarks.
  • Researchers: Open problems include geospatial surface comparison at survey-grade accuracy, autonomous navigation in unstructured construction environments, proportional liability allocation for AI-dispatched robots, and privacy-preserving settlement under EU AML constraints.
