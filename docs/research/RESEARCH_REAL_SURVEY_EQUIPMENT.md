# Real Survey Equipment for Construction Marketplace

> Research compiled 2026-03-28. Every spec and price sourced from manufacturer sites, authorized dealers, and industry publications.

## Aerial Platforms

### 1. DJI Matrice 350 RTK + Zenmuse L2 (LiDAR Config)

- **Type:** Quad-rotor, enterprise survey drone
- **Drone specs:** 6.47 kg w/ batteries, 55 min flight time (no payload), IP55, -20 to 50C, max speed 23 m/s, O3 Enterprise transmission (20 km range), RTK positioning 1 cm + 1 ppm horizontal
- **Payload — Zenmuse L2:** Frame-based LiDAR, 240K pts/s single return (1.2M multi-return), 5 returns, 450 m detection range, 4/3 CMOS RGB camera. Accuracy: 5 cm horizontal / 4 cm vertical at 150 m AGL. Covers 2.5 km2 per flight.
- **Price:** M350 RTK ~$13,600 (drone only) to ~$14,800 (combo w/ batteries). Zenmuse L2 ~$12,400-$13,500. **Total system: ~$26,000-$28,000.**
- **Operators:** Woolpert, NV5, Zeitview, hundreds of independent Part 107 operators. Most common enterprise survey drone in the US.
- **Use case:** Topographic surveys, DOT corridor mapping, stockpile volumes, construction progress, as-built verification.
- **Marketplace viable:** Yes — the workhorse. Independent operators commonly own and list this config.

### 2. DJI Matrice 350 RTK + Zenmuse P1 (Photogrammetry Config)

- **Type:** Quad-rotor, photogrammetry survey drone
- **Payload — Zenmuse P1:** Full-frame 45 MP sensor (35.9x24 mm), global mechanical shutter, interchangeable lenses (24/35/50 mm), 0.7 s interval. Covers 3 km2 per flight. Accuracy: 3 cm H / 5 cm V at 3 cm GSD.
- **Price:** Zenmuse P1 ~$6,650-$7,600. **Total system with M350: ~$20,000-$22,000.**
- **Operators:** Same as L2 config — widely used by surveying firms and independents.
- **Use case:** Orthomosaics, 3D mesh models, facade inspection, pre/post-construction documentation.
- **Marketplace viable:** Yes — lower cost entry point than LiDAR config. Very common for independent operators.

### 3. Skydio X10

- **Type:** Quad-rotor, autonomous AI-piloted drone (American-made)
- **Specs:** Six 32 MP nav cameras, 50 MP wide + 64 MP narrow cameras, FLIR Boson+ 640x512 thermal, 360-degree obstacle avoidance, 40 min flight time, 45 mph max speed, Septentrio Mosaic X5 RTK module. Survey accuracy: <1.5 in RMS without GCPs, sub-inch with GCPs.
- **Price:** ~$16,000-$20,000 depending on config. Military bulk orders ~$17,300/unit.
- **Operators:** Government agencies, DOTs, utilities. Growing adoption in commercial construction.
- **Use case:** Autonomous site inspection, bridge/structure inspection (AI obstacle avoidance), BVLOS corridor surveys. Strong in GPS-challenged environments.
- **Marketplace viable:** Yes — particularly attractive for operators who market autonomous/AI capabilities. US-made is a differentiator for government contracts.

### 4. WingtraOne Gen II (VTOL Fixed-Wing)

- **Type:** VTOL fixed-wing mapping drone
- **Specs:** 59 min flight time, 16 m/s cruise, 130-500 ha per flight depending on altitude. Payloads: Sony RX1R II (42 MP), Sony a7R IV (61 MP), MicaSense RedEdge-MX. PPK GNSS (no GCPs needed). 3 cm accuracy.
- **Price:** ~$30,000+ (historical; current pricing requires dealer quote).
- **Operators:** Woolpert, Surdex, DOT survey contractors. Fewer independents due to higher price and complexity.
- **Use case:** Large corridor surveys (highways, pipelines, rail), mine site mapping, large parcel topographic.
- **Marketplace viable:** Possible — suited for operators specializing in large-area mapping. Less common among solo operators.

### 5. senseFly eBee X (AgEagle / EagleNXT)

- **Type:** Fixed-wing hand-launched survey drone
- **Specs:** 45.7 in wingspan, 2.2-3.1 lb, 55-90 min flight (endurance mode), up to 500 ha at 120 m, RTK/PPK capable, 3 cm absolute accuracy without GCPs, GSD down to 1.5 cm. Cameras: S.O.D.A. 3D, Aeria X, Duet T.
- **Price:** ~$20,000-$30,000 (varies by camera config; dealer quote required).
- **Operators:** Woolpert (confirmed user), agricultural survey firms, DOT contractors.
- **Use case:** Large-area corridor mapping, agriculture, environmental monitoring. Belly-landing design is rugged for field use.
- **Marketplace viable:** Moderate — fixed-wing requires more skill and open space. Niche but real operator base.

### 6. Autel EVO II Pro RTK V3

- **Type:** Quad-rotor, compact survey drone
- **Specs:** 1-inch 20 MP sensor (Sony IMX383), 6K video, RTK: 1 cm + 1 ppm horizontal, 36 min flight, 1,250 g, 360-degree obstacle avoidance (19 sensor groups), 15 km transmission range.
- **Price:** ~$2,800-$3,800 with RTK. Significantly cheaper than DJI M350 system.
- **Operators:** Independent surveyors, small firms, budget-conscious operators.
- **Use case:** Small-to-medium site surveys, construction progress, residential land surveys. Good entry-level RTK drone.
- **Marketplace viable:** Yes — ideal for independent operators entering the market. Low barrier to entry.

## Ground Robots

### 7. Boston Dynamics Spot + Leica BLK ARC

- **Type:** Quadruped robot + autonomous laser scanner
- **Spot specs:** ~32 kg, 90 min battery, IP54, -20 to 45C, 1.6 m/s max speed, 14 kg payload capacity, autonomous navigation with Autowalk.
- **BLK ARC specs:** LiDAR range 0.5-25 m, GrandSLAM (LiDAR SLAM + Visual SLAM + IMU), 360-degree panoramic imagery, 24 hr scanning (compressed). IP54.
- **Price:** Spot Explorer Kit ~$75,000. BLK ARC ~$46,000. **Total system: ~$121,000-$150,000+** with software, arm, and accessories. Can exceed $195,000 fully loaded.
- **Operators:** Large ENR-ranked contractors, facility owners, specialized robotics service firms. Sold through Leica/Hexagon and Boston Dynamics dealer networks.
- **Use case:** Autonomous interior reality capture, construction progress documentation, digital twin creation for industrial facilities, BIM verification.
- **Marketplace viable:** Limited for independents due to cost. More likely listed by specialized robotics-as-a-service firms. High-value listings.

### 8. Leica Pegasus TRK (Vehicle-Mounted Mobile Mapping)

- **Type:** Vehicle-mounted mobile mapping system (not a robot per se)
- **Variants:** TRK100 (14 kg, compact), TRK300 (mid-range), TRK Neo 500/700 (730 m range), TRK Evo (highest density)
- **Specs:** Up to 4M pts/sec, 21 hr continuous operation, SmartFusion camera (up to 120 MP), integrates with IDS GPR for subsurface capture. AI-based privacy blurring.
- **Price:** Estimated $150,000-$350,000+ depending on variant (dealer quote required).
- **Operators:** DOT survey contractors, highway engineering firms, asset management companies.
- **Use case:** Highway corridor mapping, road surface assessment, asset inventory, rail surveys. Drive-through data capture at 60 mph.
- **Marketplace viable:** No for independent operators. Yes for fleet-based survey firms offering mobile mapping services.

## Inspection Robots

### 9. Flyability ELIOS 3

- **Type:** Caged indoor/confined-space inspection drone
- **Specs:** 12.5 min flight (no payload), 9.1 min with LiDAR, 4K camera + thermal + LiDAR, 180-degree FOV, 16K lumen dust-proof lighting, IP44, FlyAware SLAM navigation, cage recovers from flips. NVidia GPU onboard for real-time 3D mapping.
- **Price:** ~$40,000-$55,000+ (Elios 2 was ~$40K; Elios 3 higher. Exact pricing via sales team).
- **Operators:** Bridge inspection teams, power plant inspectors, confined space specialists.
- **Use case:** Interior bridge box-girder inspection, boiler/tank inspection, tunnel pre-survey, post-disaster structural assessment.
- **Marketplace viable:** Yes — confined space inspection is a high-value niche. Operators charge $2,000-$5,000/day.

### 10. Gecko Robotics TOKA Family

- **Type:** Magnetic wall-climbing inspection robots
- **Variants:** TOKA 3 (8 sensors, piping), TOKA 4 (24-32 sensors, boiler walls), TOKA 4 GZ (96 sensors, tank floors), TOKA Flex (12 UT probes, adapts flat-to-6in pipe).
- **Specs:** 9-18 m/min speed, phased-array UT + eddy current + acoustic + HD cameras + LiDAR. TOKA Flex covers 30+ sq ft/min, climbs 23 m (75 ft).
- **Price:** Not publicly sold — Gecko operates as a vertically integrated service company. Raised $347M total (unicorn as of 2025).
- **Operators:** Gecko Robotics only (service model). Clients include US Steel, Navy ($71M contract), Siemens Energy.
- **Use case:** Steel infrastructure inspection, ship hull inspection, power plant boiler walls, storage tank floors.
- **Marketplace viable:** No — vertically integrated. Not available for third-party operators.

### 11. Eddyfi Robotics VersaTrax

- **Type:** NDT inspection crawlers (modular, multi-terrain)
- **Variants:** VersaTrax NDT (general), P-Series (1 km pipeline runs, 68 kg payload), I-Series (50 mm openings).
- **Specs:** 60 m depth rating, 100 m tether, UT/ECA/ACFM/RVI capable, FHD camera with 10x optical zoom, ICON controller software.
- **Price:** ~$50,000-$150,000+ depending on variant and NDT tooling (dealer quote required).
- **Operators:** NDT inspection firms, pipeline operators, refinery maintenance contractors.
- **Use case:** Pipeline interior inspection, pressure vessel inspection, underwater structure inspection.
- **Marketplace viable:** Limited — specialized NDT firms only. Possible for niche marketplace listings.

### 12. Invert Robotics (Climbing Inspection Robots)

- **Type:** Vacuum-adhesion wall-climbing robot
- **Specs:** Detects defects down to 65 microns, 5 kg payload, fits 1.2 m diameter spaces, 12 cm/s speed, 1080p camera with 30x optical zoom, UT wall thickness measurement. Works on stainless steel, aluminum, plastic (6 mm+ wall thickness).
- **Price:** Service-based model (not typically sold outright).
- **Operators:** Invert Robotics (NZ-based, global operations). Focus: food/beverage tanks, wind turbine blades, chemical storage.
- **Use case:** Structural surface inspection where human access is dangerous. Less relevant to construction survey, more to industrial asset inspection.
- **Marketplace viable:** No — service model.

### 13. Emesent Hovermap ST-X

- **Type:** Modular SLAM LiDAR payload (drone, backpack, handheld, vehicle)
- **Specs:** 0.5-300 m range, 640K pts/s single return (1.92M multi-return), 360x290 degree FOV, 1.57 kg, IP65 (pending). Accuracy: +/-5 to +/-15 mm depending on environment. Enables autonomous flight in GPS-denied environments.
- **Price:** ~$40,000-$60,000 (estimate based on market positioning; dealer quote required).
- **Operators:** Mining companies, tunnel engineers, underground utility survey firms.
- **Use case:** Underground mine mapping, tunnel inspection, GPS-denied construction sites (parking structures, bridge undersides), forestry.
- **Marketplace viable:** Yes — operators who specialize in GPS-denied or underground environments.

### Pure-Play Robotic Survey Firms

Most "drone survey companies" are traditional geospatial firms that added drones. Truly pure-play robotic firms are rare:

- **Zeitview** — closest to a pure-play drone inspection network
- **Gecko Robotics** — pure robotic inspection, but vertically integrated (not a marketplace participant)
- **Exyn Technologies** — autonomous drone mapping for GPS-denied, but sells hardware + software, not services
- **Skydio** — hardware company, not an operator
