The Problem

GCs wait 2-3 weeks for survey data.
During peak season, that means missed bids worth $5-20M each.

Meanwhile, 368K drone operators have idle equipment. The industry runs on phone calls and rolodexes -- no marketplace, no bidding, no automation.

How GCs buy surveys today: 9 steps, 10-15 business days

  1. GC receives RFP or identifies project at MDOT letting
  2. Estimator reviews plan sheets -- cross-sections, alignment, existing conditions
  3. Determines survey needs: existing data rarely covers the site adequately
  4. Calls 1-3 survey firms they've used before (phone + voicemail)
  5. Survey firm quotes $3K-$15K, turnaround 5-10 business days
  6. 2-person crew goes to field with total station/GPS rovers for 1-3 days
  7. Data processed in survey firm's office: 3-5 additional business days
  8. Estimator imports into HeavyBid or Civil 3D for quantity takeoff
  9. Total elapsed: 10-15 business days -- if the crew is even available

Source: GC discovery interviews, MDOT bid analysis (43 real RFPs)

Why Now

Three converging forces that didn't exist together before 2025.

AI Agents

MCP protocol enables agent-mediated procurement. AI translates "I need topo for a highway bid" into robot task specs, evaluates bids across robot types, and assembles deliverable packages. McKinsey: agentic procurement delivers 19-21% lower costs, 58% shorter cycles.

Drone Maturity

DJI Matrice 350 RTK + Zenmuse L2 delivers survey-grade LiDAR at $26K -- a price point that makes independent operator businesses viable. 5cm accuracy, 4.2M points/sec. The workhorse is ready.

Regulatory Clarity

Part 107 is mature (368K holders, 84% pass rate). Part 108 BVLOS rules are finalizing. ERC-8004 + x402 give robots on-chain identity and payment rails. The legal framework caught up.

The infrastructure layers (MCP robot servers, on-chain robot identity, stablecoin payments) matured in 2025. The marketplace layer for physical robot tasks does not yet exist. The window is open.
