The Problem

You win a $14M highway contract.
Field conditions don't match plan sheets.
You need a re-survey in 48 hours
to avoid a $500K change order.

Your usual survey crew is booked for 3 weeks.

This is the real pain: not routine surveys (you plan around those), but the emergency re-survey when schedule and dollars are on the line. Today, there is no fast way to find a certified operator with the right equipment who is available this week.

How GCs buy surveys today: 9 steps, 10-15 business days

  1. GC receives RFP or identifies project at MDOT letting
  2. Estimator reviews plan sheets -- cross-sections, alignment, existing conditions
  3. Determines survey needs: existing data rarely covers the site adequately
  4. Calls 1-3 survey firms they've used before (phone + voicemail)
  5. Survey firm quotes $3K-$15K, turnaround 5-10 business days
  6. 2-person crew goes to field with total station/GPS rovers for 1-3 days
  7. Data processed in survey firm's office: 3-5 additional business days
  8. Estimator imports into HeavyBid or Civil 3D for quantity takeoff
  9. Total elapsed: 10-15 business days -- if the crew is even available

Source: MDOT bid analysis (43 real RFPs from 2024-2026 letting schedule)

Why construction surveying?

Survey is the first spend on every construction project. It is standardized (MDOT has published specs), repeat-purchase (progress monitoring is monthly), and underserved by technology. GCs outsource it because they must -- none of the 15 Michigan GCs we researched have in-house drone survey capability.

Liability & Compliance

The #1 question GCs will ask:
"If the survey is wrong, who pays?"

Construction surveys in Michigan require a Professional Land Surveyor (PLS) stamp. Our marketplace must handle the professional liability chain -- this is non-negotiable.

Operator

Carries drone aviation insurance. Flies the site, collects raw data. Part 107 certified.

→

PLS (Surveyor)

Michigan-licensed PLS stamps the deliverable. Carries E&O insurance. Legally responsible for accuracy.

→

Platform (YAK)

Verifies all insurance + certifications. Holds escrow. Carries platform E&O + cyber liability. Provides audit trail.

→

GC (Buyer)

Receives PLS-stamped deliverable with E&O coverage they can flow claims through -- same as hiring a survey firm directly.

Key requirement: Named PLS on every deliverable

Not "PLS-affiliated." An actual Michigan-licensed Professional Land Surveyor who stamps the final product and whose E&O insurance backs it. The platform verifies this before any deliverable ships.

Deliverable specs in GC language

LandXML or .TIN for HeavyBid. DWG with Civil 3D feature codes matching MDOT standards. DTM (not DSM) for quantity takeoff. Cross-sections at 50-foot stations. MDOT Geodetic Survey Section compliance.
